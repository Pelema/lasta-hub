The department concerned should conduct an extensive market survey to determine the extent and the nature of the need. After PDU has allocated a code to the proposed programme, it will communicate the code to the department. The department has to use this code every time they are working on programme. Select the appropriate programme code to resume the need analysis process.

The Department will draft a survey instrument in consultation with PDU. The survey instrument must be approved by PDU before it is send to industry and stakeholders. The Department should load all the necessary information e.g. organisation name, date they were consulted and their response. Before Drafting

PAC should mainly consist of representatives from local industries, academic advisory board and peers from higher education institutions, locally and abroad. Maximum PAC members should not exceed twenty persons (20). For professional qualifications, the concerned professional regulatory bodies should be represented on the PAC. The names, contact details and appointment letters of all the appointed CDC will be loaded on (Appoint CDC). The names, contact details and appointment letters of all PAC members will be loaded on (Appoint PAC).

Draft Preparation

This is the last stage for curriculum development between CDC and PDU. Ones PDU approve the curriculum of the programme, the document will be loaded on (Recommendations). This signifies that the draft curriculum is now ready to be distributed to PAC members.

The NQA will write a Registration Report on issues of concern (Recommendations). PDU will respond and make the necessary corrections and resubmit the final documents (Registration).
