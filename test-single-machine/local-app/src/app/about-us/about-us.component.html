ABOUT PDU

The Programme Development Unit (PDU) is academic supporting unit is responsible for leading, coordinating and managing all programme development activities (both new and revised programmes) up to the point of registration of the resultant qualifications on the National Qualifications Framework (NQF).

ROLES AND FUNCTIONS:

The following are the roles and functions of the unit:

  • Develop, publish, and review the curriculum framework of the institution, including teaching, learning and assessment frameworks, Policy of Programme Development, Policy on Short courses, Double and Joint degree Policy and Industry Credential Courses Policy.
  • Lead, advice and support academic departments in curriculum development of all programmes and short courses.
  • Facilitate the quality assurance of all programmes to align to the National, Professional and Institutional standards.
  • Facilitating registration of qualifications on the NQF;
  • Assisting with verification of qualification data in the Yearbooks and on ITS.
  • Conduct developmental training sessions and capacity building intervention for academics on curriculum development issues
  • Facilitate the Programme Qualifications Mix (PQM) that reflect NUST mandate and key attributes that reflect a world class university that focus on UN-SDGs and 4IR.

OUR VISION

To be a port of call that promote excellence through technological, innovative and sustainable programme development practices at NUST.

OUR MISSION

To facilitate the development of programmes and qualifications applying internationally benchmarked best practices in response to national needs.

OUR VALUES

  • Accountability: We take full responsibility for all our decisions and actions.
  • Excellence: We strive for quality and professionalism in all our actions.
  • Teamwork : We foster a culture of teamwork in pursuit of our mission.
  • Systematic : We strive for consistency in all our activities.
  • Integrity: We ensure honesty and moral principles in our processes and systems.
